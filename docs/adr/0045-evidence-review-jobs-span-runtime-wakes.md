# Evidence Review Jobs Span Runtime Wakes

Status: accepted

XiaoBa represents review of a fixed Evidence Bundle as a durable Evidence Review Job that may span Runtime Learning wakes. The Evidence Bundle is an immutable manifest over content-addressed Evidence Shards rather than one embedded prompt payload. Wakes schedule independently resumable Review Quanta and stop admitting new work at their scheduling boundary; they do not reject a candidate because of estimated prompt size or require the complete review to fit one heartbeat. Evidence coverage and completed quanta survive restart, while the final Capability Transition remains atomic. This accepts durable coordination, storage, and recovery complexity so future evidence can grow beyond one model context or wake without whole-job retries, permanent oversized queues, duplicated large payloads, or silent evidence loss.

The Runtime owns Deterministic Evidence Sharding. It partitions first along stable domain boundaries such as episodes, evidence windows, turns, Referenced Skills, and Registry records; an oversized domain unit is split recursively along its own stable structure, with a stable byte boundary only as the final fallback. Models consume the fixed shards and cannot redefine the evidence boundary. This preserves reproducible shard identity and prevents model-selected chunking from silently excluding contradictory material.

Every Evidence Shard receives Dual-Lane Evidence Coverage. Independent Author and Verifier reader lanes produce separate provenance-linked Evidence Dossiers from the same immutable shards and structured schema; they do not share natural-language findings. The Runtime creates a deterministic Dossier Difference Index for the Verifier, but does not resolve semantic disagreements itself. A Capability Transition cannot commit until both lanes cover the complete manifest. This approximately doubles evidence-reading work in exchange for removing a shared-summary failure point and making disagreement auditable.

Each lane-shard read completes with an authoritative Shard Finding Set: a coverage disposition plus typed findings linked to exact spans in that immutable shard. The Runtime validates hashes and span bounds. Free-form explanation is diagnostic only, model-reported confidence is not an admission signal, and a Reader cannot propose a Capability Transition or cite outside the fixed manifest. An unreadable or ambiguous result does not satisfy coverage and requires retry, deterministic re-sharding, or an explicitly stronger reader.

Reader lanes remain isolated until both Evidence Dossiers are complete. The final Skill Verifier then receives the Skill Draft, both Dossiers, the structural Difference Index, and bounded access to original shards. The Runtime converts contradictions, limits, risks, source instructions, privilege implications, unresolved questions, classification differences, and uncorroborated draft claims into Review Obligations. The Verifier must disposition every obligation with original shard spans before a Capability Transition may commit; unresolved semantics defer the job rather than triggering runtime adjudication or unbounded model voting.

An Evidence Review Job persists an Evidence Review Dependency Graph rather than a mutable linear phase. Each Review Quantum has a content-derived identity over its kind, input hashes, and reviewer or prompt version; it becomes schedulable only after declared dependencies succeed. Workers claim nodes through expiring Quantum Leases, successful results are idempotent, and provider failures retry only the affected node. Job disposition is derived from the graph except for explicit active, deferred, completed, or terminal-failed outcomes. This adds dependency and lease machinery in exchange for parallel coverage, local retry, and crash recovery without whole-job replay.

The Runtime Learning Heartbeat remains the sole Review Quantum scheduler. Each wake computes runnable nodes, claims a bounded set of leases within reviewer concurrency, stops claiming at its scheduling boundary, lets claimed quanta finish within their own deadlines, and requests a follow-up wake when runnable work remains. No independent persistent Review Worker loop is introduced. This keeps scheduling, ownership, coalescing, and graceful drain within the existing Runtime boundary while allowing jobs to advance across arbitrarily many wakes.

Runnable work uses durable, work-conserving round-robin at two levels: first across operational recovery, live learning, historical learning, and semantic reassessment classes, then across jobs in the selected class. A pass claims at most one quantum per runnable job before cycling again; a sole runnable job may consume spare capacity. Inside a job, ready commit, final-verifier, author, dossier-construction, and reader nodes follow critical-path priority, with Author and Verifier reader lanes balanced. Job size and retry history never create permanent priority.

Each job fixes an immutable Review Basis over its Evidence Bundle manifest, relevant Registry read set, Referenced Skill hashes, and Review Policy Snapshot. The final transition uses a Review Commit Fence: an atomic version-vector comparison commits only if all declared dependencies still match. A relevant change ordered before the fence creates a Successor Review Job and reuses successful quanta whose content-identified inputs remain valid; a change ordered after the fence creates later reassessment work. This provides a serializable ordering without mutating in-flight evidence, globally locking Registry state, discarding reusable work, or allowing continuous evidence arrival to starve every commit.
